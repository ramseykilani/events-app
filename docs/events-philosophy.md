# Events — Design Philosophy

---

## Abundance, Not Scarcity

Most apps are built on a scarcity mindset. They assume you won't come back unless they drag you back — so they notify, badge, streak, and guilt you into returning. Events rejects this entirely.

Events is built for people in an **abundance frame**. People who open the app because they *want* to, not because a push notification made them feel like they were missing something. The app provides value by reducing friction between you and the people around you. It doesn't need to manufacture urgency because the value is real and self-evident.

Think of it like the flashlight app on your phone. It doesn't have a social feed. It doesn't gamify your usage. It just works. You open it when you need light, and you close it when you don't. Events operates on the same principle.

---

## Anti-Engagement

No scores. No numbers. No read receipts. No engagement optimization of any kind.

Optimizing for engagement is an implicit admission that you're not providing real value and need to steal attention instead. Events doesn't steal attention. It serves a purpose. If you don't open the app for a month, nothing happens. No guilt. No re-engagement campaign. Nothing.

The one exception is person-triggered notifications. When someone adds you to an event, you receive a notification — not because Events wants your attention, but because another person is trying to tell you something. The app is just the delivery mechanism. The information is real, the trigger is human, and the app has no stake in whether you tap it. This is categorically different from the app manufacturing urgency on its own behalf.

---

## Anti-Product Thinking

There is no bait. There is no hook. There is no retention strategy beyond being useful.

The assumption is that the target user — someone already living in abundance, already going to events, already coordinating with friends — will find a clingy, attention-seeking app repulsive rather than engaging. So Events doesn't cling. It sits quietly on your phone, does its job when you need it, and otherwise leaves you completely alone.

---

## A Tool, Not a Platform

Events is a tool for people in a network, not a platform trying to boost a founder's ego or climb an app store hierarchy. We are not trying to make money. We are not trying to be the top of any hierarchy. We are not trying to own anyone's attention.

The tools of the new age need to be exactly that — tools. Things that reduce friction between people, that serve the user rather than extract from them. Events exists to make one specific thing in your life slightly easier, and that is the entire ambition.

---

*If it's actually useful, you'll use it. If it's not, no amount of dark patterns will save it.*
